# Research Proposal Template

Applicable to: Undergraduate Thesis, Master's Thesis, Academic Paper

---

## 1. Research Title
> Clear, accurately reflects the content, includes key terms. Typically 15–25 words.

**Example:** The Impact of Service Quality on Customer Satisfaction at Commercial Banks in Vietnam

---

## 2. Rationale / Justification
> Answer: Why is this topic important? What practical problem exists? What is the research gap?

Suggested structure (3–4 paragraphs):
- Practical context (figures, current situation)
- Existing problem / unresolved issue
- Gap in academic research
- Reason the researcher chose this topic

---

## 3. Research Objectives

**General Objective:**
> One sentence describing the overall purpose of the study.

**Specific Objectives:**
1. [Objective 1 — typically to identify/measure]
2. [Objective 2 — typically to analyze/evaluate]
3. [Objective 3 — typically to propose/recommend]

---

## 4. Research Questions
> Each question should correspond to one specific objective.

1. [Question 1]
2. [Question 2]
3. [Question 3]

---

## 5. Research Subject and Scope

- **Research subject:** [The phenomenon, relationship, or issue being studied]
- **Research participants / respondents:** [The people/organizations providing data]
- **Geographic scope:** [Location]
- **Time scope:** [Data collection period]

---

## 6. Research Methodology

- **Research approach:** Quantitative / Qualitative / Mixed Methods
- **Research design:** Descriptive / Explanatory / Exploratory / Experimental
- **Data collection method:** Survey / Interview / Observation / Document Analysis
- **Analysis method:** [Descriptive statistics, regression, factor analysis, content analysis, etc.]

---

## 7. Expected Contributions

- **Theoretical:** [What theory/model will be added to, tested, or extended?]
- **Practical:** [Who will use the findings? How will they be applied?]

---

## 8. Proposed Structure

- Chapter 1: Research Overview
- Chapter 2: Theoretical Background and Research Model
- Chapter 3: Research Methodology
- Chapter 4: Results and Discussion
- Chapter 5: Conclusions and Recommendations
