# Research Writing Review Checklist

Use this to review a research document section by section. Mark ✅ if satisfactory, ⚠️ if needs improvement, ❌ if missing/incorrect.

---

## A. Overall Document

- [ ] Title accurately reflects the research content
- [ ] Objectives — research questions — methodology — results — conclusions are consistent with each other
- [ ] The paper answers the research questions as originally posed
- [ ] No contradictions between sections
- [ ] Language is academic, objective, and non-emotional

---

## B. Introduction

- [ ] Context and justification clearly stated
- [ ] Research gap identified
- [ ] Research objectives are clear and specific
- [ ] Research questions are answerable
- [ ] Scope of the study is clearly defined

---

## C. Literature Review / Theoretical Background

- [ ] Key concepts are clearly defined (with citations)
- [ ] Sources are sufficiently diverse (not relying on just 1–2 references)
- [ ] Sources are reasonably current (prefer within last 10 years)
- [ ] Literature is analyzed and synthesized — not merely listed
- [ ] Research gap is logically argued based on the review
- [ ] Theoretical framework / research model is explained and justified

---

## D. Research Methodology

- [ ] Method is appropriate for the objectives and research questions
- [ ] Described in sufficient detail to allow replication
- [ ] Sample size and sampling method are explained
- [ ] Data collection instruments are clearly described (questionnaire, interviews, etc.)
- [ ] Analysis method is appropriate for the type of data
- [ ] Limitations of the method are acknowledged

---

## E. Results

- [ ] Results directly address the research questions
- [ ] Data is clearly presented (tables and figures have captions)
- [ ] No jump to conclusions without supporting evidence
- [ ] Figures and statistics are accurate and consistent throughout
- [ ] Results and interpretations are clearly distinguished

---

## F. Discussion

- [ ] Meaning of the results is explained
- [ ] Results are compared with previous studies (agreement or divergence)
- [ ] Unexpected results are explained
- [ ] Limitations of the study are acknowledged

---

## G. Conclusion

- [ ] Summarizes the answers to the research questions
- [ ] States theoretical and practical contributions
- [ ] Recommendations are grounded in the research findings
- [ ] Directions for future research are suggested
- [ ] No new information is introduced that was not present in earlier sections

---

## H. References

- [ ] All in-text citations appear in the reference list
- [ ] All references in the list are cited in the text
- [ ] Citation format is consistent (APA / MLA / Vancouver...)
- [ ] No fabricated or unverifiable references

---

## 🔴 Common Errors to Avoid

1. **Objectives and conclusions do not align** — the conclusion must directly answer the stated objectives
2. **Literature review is merely a list** — needs synthesis, comparison, and identification of commonalities/differences
3. **Vague methodology** — must be detailed enough for others to understand what was done
4. **Conclusions exceed the data** — do not conclude beyond what the data actually supports
5. **Missing research gap** — must argue why this study is necessary
