# Topic Idea Evaluation Checklist

Use this checklist to evaluate a research topic idea before investing significant time into it.

---

## ✅ 1. Feasibility

- [ ] Can data be collected within the available time frame?
- [ ] Are the necessary resources available (funding, equipment, access to respondents)?
- [ ] Is the scope appropriate for the academic level (undergraduate / master's / doctoral)?
- [ ] Does the researcher have sufficient background knowledge to carry it out?

## ✅ 2. Novelty

- [ ] How does this topic differ from existing studies?
- [ ] Is there a clear research gap?
- [ ] Does the research context (time, location, population) introduce something new?

## ✅ 3. Significance

- [ ] What theoretical contribution will the research make (to theory, models)?
- [ ] Does it have practical implications (policy, business, society)?
- [ ] Who would be interested in the findings?

## ✅ 4. Research Question

- [ ] Is the research question answerable (not too broad, not too narrow)?
- [ ] Is the question focused on a specific problem?
- [ ] Can it be measured or explored using real-world data?

---

## 🔴 Warning Signs to Reconsider

- Topic is too broad: "Research on Vietnamese education" → needs to be narrowed
- Topic has already been heavily studied with no new angle
- Necessary data cannot be accessed
- Research question is purely descriptive (only asks "what" without asking "why" or "how")

---

## 💡 Tips for Narrowing a Topic

Formula: **[Subject/Population] + [Phenomenon/Problem] + [Specific Context]**

Examples:
- ❌ "Research on online buying behavior"
- ✅ "The Influence of Online Product Reviews on Purchase Decisions Among University Students in Ho Chi Minh City"
